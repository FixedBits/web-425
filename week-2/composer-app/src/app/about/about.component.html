Ludwig van Beethoven

Ludwig van Beethoven was a famous German composer who was born on December 17, 1770, in Bonn, Germany, and passed away on March 26, 1827, in Vienna, Austria. He played a crucial role in the transition from the Classical to the Romantic era in music. Beethoven is considered one of the greatest music composers ever. His music was inspired by the classical traditions of Joseph Haydn and Mozart, but he also incorporated new ideas related to humanism and nationalism. Some of his well-known works include the “Archduke Trio”, “Battle Symphony”, “Eroica Symphony”, and “Moonlight Sonata”. Source
